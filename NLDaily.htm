Left fielder Wally Moon slammed 2 homeruns and had 5 RBI and Don Drysdale
completed the game without help from the bullpen as the Los Angeles Dodgers
defeated the St. Louis Cardinals by a score of 9 to 5 at Busch Stadium.
 
Moon enjoyed a big day at the plate.  He clubbed a two-run home run in the 1st
inning, delivered a single in the 3rd inning and popped a two-run clout over
the fence in the 4th inning.  Los Angeles finished with 13 hits in the
victory.  
 
Drysdale(3-0) allowed 5 runs on the game. Gary Blaylock(1-1) was the losing
pitcher.  He allowed a few too many baserunners giving up 12 hits and 5 walks
in 5 innings.  
 
'I just got in the box, looked for a pitch and swung,' Moon said.  'That's it'

pitcher was Don Newcombe(0-1).  He was hit hard and gave up 9 hits and 2

Ed Bouchee had 2 base hits and 2 RBI and Don Cardwell carried a no-hitter into
the 6th inning as the Philadelphia Phillies topped the Pittsburgh Pirates by
a score of 4 to 2 at Shibe Park.
 
Cardwell(1-1) pitched well for Philadelphia in their victory.  He went 6
innings allowing 4 hits and 2 walks dropping his ERA from 5.19 to 4.30.  The
right-handed Cardwell lost his no-hit bid when Don Hoak singled with nobody
out in the 6th inning.  The Pittsburgh offense proved no trouble in the 9th.
 
Cardwell got help from Taylor Phillips who was credited with his 1st save.
Vern Law(2-1) was tagged with the loss.  He pitched 7 innings and surrendered
8 hits and 1 walk.
 
'Wow.  That's about all I can think to say.' said Eddie Sawyer following the
victory.  'If ever a team needed a win, it was us.'

loss.  He served up 3 gopher balls in 4 innings.

losing ways and their record is now 4-10, worst in the league.  They are 2-8
in their last ten games.
