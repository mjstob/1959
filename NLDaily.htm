CHICAGO AT SAN FRANCISCO
Cubs............ 0 0 0  2 0 1  1 1 1  -  6 12  1
Giants.......... 0 0 0  1 0 0  1 2 4  -  8 10  1
Win:Worthington(3-1) Loss:Elston(4-4)
Homeruns- E.Banks(18th), D.Long(4th), O.Cepeda(11th)
 
Orlando Cepeda cleared the bases with his grand slam as the San Francisco
Giants rallied impressively to win in dramatic style over Chicago Cubs, 8 to
6.
 
San Francisco came through with the game-winning runs in the bottom of the 9th
inning when they scored 4 runs on 1 hit.  The key at-bat was by Cepeda who
brought a roar from the partisan Seals Stadium fans when he launched a
rocket-shot out of the park for a Grand Slam homerun.  Even though they
scored in 5 different innings, Chicago was not able to pull off the win.
 
The win was credited to Al Worthington(3-1) who went 1 inning allowing 1 run.
The loss was charged to Don Elston(4-4) in relief.  
 
Worthington and Cepeda were really whooping it up in the clubhouse after the
great come from behind victory.  As Bill Rigney noted, 'A win like this one
can do wonders for a team's attitude.' Everyone shares in the joy - the fans,
the players - even you guys - it's better than winning by a dozen runs!'

Bob Buhl reeled off a total of 14 strikeouts as the Milwaukee Braves beat the
Philadelphia Phillies in 11 innings by a score of 7 to 6.
 
The score was tied at 6 after nine.  Milwaukee was victorious in the 11th
inning.  After an out was recorded, Mickey Vernon delivered a solo blast
making Milwaukee the victors to the delight of the home town faithful.  This
was a game of many highs and lows for the home town fans, as the lead kept
changing - 6 times in all.  
 
Buhl(3-2) picked up the complete game victory, allowing 6 runs. Jack
Meyer(1-1) was tagged with the loss in relief.  
 
Everyone seemed to enjoy the performance of Buhl - except Philadelphia.  Eddie
Sawyer lamented, 'He just was 'on' this afternoon.  He was keeping our guys
off balance all game.  A fine game by a good strikeout pitcher - makes it a
fun game for the hometown fans - unfortunately we were the visitors!'

Humberto Robinson allowed Eddie Sawyer to rest his bullpen, tossing a complete
game at County Stadium where the Philadelphia Phillies beat the Milwaukee
Braves by the score of 11 to 2.
 
Ed Bouchee was a source of great heartache for the Milwaukee faithful all day.
He just kept pounding out hits! He tripled plating a run in the 4th inning,
cleared the fences for a two-run home run in the 8th inning and doubled
bringing in two baserunners in the 9th inning.  Philadelphia rapped out 13
hits for the afternoon.  
 
The winning pitcher was Robinson(2-1) who allowed 2 runs for the game. Warren
Spahn(6-4) was given the loss.  Philadelphia scored big getting 9 runs off
him in 8 and 1/3 innings.  
 
'I just got in the box, looked for a pitch and swung,' Bouchee said.  'That's
it'

the Cincinnati Reds by the count of 13 to 4.

scored a run in the 9th inning.  Pittsburgh totaled 17 hits on the afternoon.

Bob Purkey(2-6) took the loss.  'Thankfully we got ourselves a win', Law
said.  'It's great to be back on the winning side of things.'
