Homeruns- E.Banks(28th), H.Anderson(6th)
 
Right fielder Lee Walls had 2 base hits as the Chicago Cubs beat the
Philadelphia Phillies by a score of 5 to 3.
 
Chicago tallied 3 runs in the top of the 6th inning with the help of 3 hits.
Walls started the inning off right when he drew a walk.  Ernie Banks struck
out, unable to help the rally.  Dale Long then drew a walk.  One out later,
Al Dark came to the plate and lined a one-base hit plating a run.  Sammy
Taylor came up to bat and stroked a base-hit plating one more runner.  Elmer
Singleton extended the rally and delivered a single scoring the final run of
the inning.  Philadelphia threatened in the 9th but Chicago was able to put
down the rally.  
 
Singleton(2-0) got credit for the victory, pitching 8 and 1/3 innings and
allowing 3 runs. Don Elston recorded the save, his 7th.  Ray Semproch(2-8)
ended up with the loss in relief.  
 
'I always am most impressed when a pitcher finds a way to win even without his
best stuff,' Bob Scheffing said after the game.  'Singleton did not have
command of his pitches most of the afternoon, but he proved he really knew
how to pitch!'
