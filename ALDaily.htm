It was a great finish at Kansas City as the Kansas City Athletics pulled off a
dramatic comeback beating the Baltimore Orioles by the count of 7 to
6.
 
Kansas City came up with the deciding runs in the 9th inning scoring 2 runs
using 2 hits.  Roger Maris got things going when he laced a one-base hit.
Milt Pappas then threw a wild pitch.  Bob Cerv was next to bat, but he was a
strikeout victim.  After another out, Preston Ward came to bat and drew a
walk.  Harry Chiti came up to bat and was hit by a pitch.  Ray Boone added to
the uprising and drew a walk bringing in a run.  Bill Tuttle came next and he
laced a single resulting in an exciting win for Kansas City and a raucous
post-game celebration by the home town fans.  Baltimore was out-hit by Kansas
City, 13 hits to 8.  
 
Dick Tomanek(1-0) was the winner allowing no runs in 1 inning. The loss was
charged to Pappas(1-3).  He was touched for 12 hits and 6 walks in 8 and 2/3
innings.  
 
'Now there is a lesson in perseverance for you,' Frank House noted.  'It was
looking pretty dim there for a while, but we just kept plugging away and it
really paid off in spades! And it was great to see the crowd go crazy there
at the end!'
