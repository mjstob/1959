Right fielder Hank Aaron smacked 2 homeruns and had 5 RBI at County Stadium as
the Milwaukee Braves beat the Los Angeles Dodgers in 11 innings by the count
of 8 to 6.
 
The game was deadlocked at 6 after nine full innings.  Milwaukee ended up
winning it in the 11th inning.  After an out was recorded, Mickey Vernon was
the sparkplug when he drew a walk.  Eddie Mathews was next to bat, but he was
a strikeout victim.  Aaron then cracked a two-run shot giving Milwaukee the
win much to the delight of the home town crowd.  Milwaukee won despite being
out-hit by Los Angeles 13 hits to 8.  
 
The win went to Don McMahon(1-0) who allowed no runs in 2 innings. Larry
Sherry(2-1) absorbed the loss in relief.  Aaron was jubilant after the game
'Let me see a box-score' he joked, 'I want to see how that game looks on
paper.'
